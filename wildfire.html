Task 5

🎯 I need to create a summary table that has 4 variables: Category, area_km2 (sum of the areas of all the cells), n_cells (number of grid cells), and Probability (area_km2 divided by sum of area_km2)

#⚡ Code:
prob_tbl <- county_cats %>% 
  group_by(Category) %>% 
  summarize(
    area_km2 = sum(cell_area_km2),
    n_cells  = n(),
    .groups = "drop") %>% 
  mutate(
    Probability = area_km2/sum(area_km2)) %>% 
  select(Category, area_km2, n_cells, Probability)
# ✅  Check
print(prob_tbl)

Part B: Probability Modeling

Task 1

🎯 Using a lognormal distribution, I need to calculate the probability that a fire will spread 10 or more kilometers after 8 hours in a “High” risk zone.

#⚡ Code:
p_high_exact <- plnorm(10, meanlog = 1.907, sdlog = 0.280, lower.tail = FALSE)
# ✅  Check
print(p_high_exact)
[1] 0.0788567
p_high_exact*100
[1] 7.88567

🗣 Using a lognormal model, it appears that a fire has a 7.886% chance of burning 10 km or more after 8 hours.
